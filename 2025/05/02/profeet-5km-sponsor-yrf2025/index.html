ProFeet Podiatry backs local movement as 5km sponsor for the 2025 Yeppoon Running Festival

In Yeppoon, movement means more than fitness. It’s connection. It’s community. It’s joy.
And in 2025, that spirit is getting a big boost thanks to a local name passionate about putting people on the right path — ProFeet Podiatry, proud sponsor of the Yeppoon Running Festival’s 5km event.

“We love keeping people active, healthy and happy,” says Kate Brown, ProFeet Yeppoon’s store manager.
“What better way to support our community than to get behind an event that celebrates exactly that?”

A Partnership Rooted in Movement and Purpose

As a health-focused local business, ProFeet knows the power of staying mobile — and staying connected. This new partnership isn’t just a logo on a finish line banner. It’s a reflection of shared values: wellbeing, inclusion, encouragement, and celebrating every step of progress.

“For us, it’s about inspiring our community to embrace active lifestyles,” Kate explains.
“We love being able to offer runners and walkers real support — not just on race day, but long after.”

Who Are ProFeet Podiatry?

Nestled in the heart of Yeppoon, ProFeet is more than just a podiatry clinic. It’s a place where science meets service — with a team of dedicated podiatrists and footwear experts who take the time to understand each individual’s needs.

Their difference? A holistic approach.
Think expert assessments, personalised care, and a retail experience that focuses on fit, form and function. Whether you’re recovering from injury, training for your first 5km, or just want shoes that don’t give you blisters, they’re here to help you move better, feel better and live better.

Oh, and if you’re after a new running route? They’ll happily recommend a few of their local favourites.

Real Runners, Real Passion

ProFeet’s expertise doesn’t come from textbooks alone. Their team lives and breathes the active lifestyle:

  • Kate is an ultra-distance runner and certified running coach
  • Emily, podiatrist, has a track background and loves 5km events
  • Susie, from retail, is a marathon runner and Ironman triathlete
  • Sharnti, also retail, thrives in track and long-distance trail runs

Their collective experience means they understand what runners go through — and how best to support them.

Helping You Run Well — and Recover Better

Whether you’re aiming for a 5km PB or just want to enjoy weekend walks without discomfort, ProFeet offers services that make a real difference:

  • Zebris treadmill gait analysis for in-depth biomechanical insights
  • Flexible custom orthotics for personalised support
  • Shockwave therapy and injury rehab for recovery
  • Expert footwear fittings and pressure mapping
  • And honest, practical advice for every level of runner

For casual joggers, they focus on comfort and injury prevention. For serious athletes, it’s about performance and fine-tuning the fit.

Why Foot Health Really Matters

It’s easy to forget the importance of your feet — until something hurts.
“Pain in your feet can affect your knees, hips, and back,” Kate says.
“It can derail your training or just make daily life uncomfortable.”

The good news? A few small steps — the right shoes, early advice, targeted treatment — can make a world of difference. It’s about making every step feel better, whether you’re running a race or chasing after your kids.

On Race Day: ProFeet Will Be There

And they’re not just supporting from the sidelines. At the 2025 event, ProFeet will be on-site with:

✅ Post-run massage and blister advice from qualified podiatrists
✅ Discount vouchers for performance footwear
✅ Injury prevention tips and fitting support
✅ Plus a welcoming space to rest, chat and recover

Looking Ahead to Their First Festival

For Kate and the team, the Yeppoon Running Festival is more than a sponsorship opportunity — it’s a chance to connect.

“We’re so excited to feel the energy on the day,” she says.
“Watching people of all abilities cross the line — whether it’s their first 5km or their fiftieth — is what it’s all about.”

Starting Your First 5km? Here’s Their Advice:

  • Build your kilometres gradually — don’t rush it
  • Follow a plan (bonus points if it’s made by a running coach)
  • Address niggles early with a podiatrist
  • Get fitted for the right shoes for your feet
  • And most importantly — have fun and enjoy the atmosphere

More Than a Clinic — A Community Partner

ProFeet doesn’t just see themselves as a business. They see themselves as part of Yeppoon’s broader health and wellness movement. From families to competitive runners, they want to help locals live their best, most active lives.

“Whether you’re a runner, a walker, or just want to feel great on your feet — we’re here for you.”

Visit the ProFeet team at the Yeppoon Running Festival this year —
get support, ask questions, grab a massage, and walk away feeling stronger.

Because at ProFeet, every step really does count.
